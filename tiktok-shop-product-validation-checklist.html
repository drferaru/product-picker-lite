The 7-point pre-sample check

1. Three-second visual proof

Can a buyer understand the benefit before the voiceover explains it? If the product only works after a long explanation, it is weak for TikTok Shop discovery.

2. One specific buyer and one specific pain

“Everyone could use this” is not validation. Name the buyer, the situation, and the annoying moment the product fixes.

3. Landed-cost margin survives reality

Do not score margin from supplier unit cost alone. Include shipping, platform fees, returns, creator commission, damaged units, and a realistic test discount.

4. Supplier clarity

A valid first sample needs clear specs, variants, MOQ, packaging, lead time, and enough listing history or supplier detail to compare alternatives.

5. Category and account risk

Avoid a first test if the product leans on medical claims, batteries, cosmetics, ingestibles, children’s safety, trademark bait, or unclear compliance documents.

6. QC and return risk

Fragile, size-sensitive, subjective, or high-defect products need more inspection before they deserve sample money.

7. Repeatable content angles

List at least three simple videos: before/after, comparison, problem demo, setup, mistake, or bundle use case. One hook is not enough.
