Fast example: supportive house slipper

  • Buyer: someone who wants a softer at-home shoe but does not want a bulky orthopedic look.
  • Visual proof: bend test, sole thickness, heel cup, outsole grip, and side-by-side with a flat slipper.
  • Risk: avoid medical promises. Keep copy around comfort, fit, and daily-use preference.
  • First test: one sample, three videos, one landing/listing draft, no bulk order until comments and clicks show interest.

Decision: worth a sample only if landed cost leaves room after shipping and returns. If the only hook is a vague comfort claim, reject it.
