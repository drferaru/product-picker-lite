The sample QC pass

Score the sample in the order that protects cash first.

CheckPass signalReject signal
Listing match Sample matches dimensions, material, color, function, and included parts. Supplier photos hide scale, missing parts, weak material, or inconsistent variants.
3-second proof A phone video can show the benefit without a long explanation. The product only sounds useful after a paragraph of copy.
Packaging and delivery Arrives clean, intact, easy to understand, and safe to ship again. Crushed packaging, bad odor, loose parts, confusing instructions, or fragile build.
Defect and return risk Normal handling does not expose obvious breakage, fit, safety, or support issues. One defect would trigger refund, bad review, injury concern, or support spiral.
Margin after reality Margin still survives landed cost, platform fees, creator/content test, returns, and replacements. Profit only works if returns, ads, creator costs, and damaged units are ignored.

Decision rule: if the product fails 3-second proof or margin-after-reality, do not solve it with more content. Kill it or change the product angle before ordering inventory.

Copyable supplier follow-up

Ask for proof before the next order.

Use this after the sample inspection to pressure-test whether the supplier can support repeatable orders without creating avoidable account or return risk.

Hi, I reviewed the sample and need confirmation before ordering more units: 1. Please confirm the exact material, dimensions, included parts, and packaging for bulk orders. 2. Please send current photos or video of the production version, not only catalog images. 3. What is the normal defect/replacement policy if units arrive damaged or inconsistent? 4. Can the product ship with the same packaging quality as the sample? 5. Are there any claims, certifications, batteries, electronics, cosmetics, medical, safety, or restricted-category issues I should know before listing? I am comparing suppliers before a small test order, so specific answers matter.

Do not paste regulated or medical claims into a listing just because the supplier used them. Keep the first test focused on visible product proof and low-risk buyer utility.
