Listing proofMain photo, scale shot, detail shot, use-case shot, variant/bundle image, and risk-disclosure photos.If photos cannot prove the promise, do not launch yet.
Content testCreator sample, UGC brief, AI video concept, editing, or the first three short-form hooks.If no hook can show the benefit fast, fix the angle before inventory.
Return reserveExpected defects, sizing/fit confusion, fragile packaging, refund friction, and customer support time.If returns erase margin, reject or reposition.
First proof batchOnly enough units to validate demand, content response, and supplier reliability.If MOQ forces a large blind buy, negotiate or walk away.
Stop-loss capThe maximum cash you are willing to spend before demand proof.If total launch budget exceeds the cap, cut scope or reject.

Simple rule: a product is not cheap because the unit cost is cheap. It is cheap only if the full test budget is small enough to lose without damaging the next test.
