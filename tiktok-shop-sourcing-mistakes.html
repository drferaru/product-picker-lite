Mistake 1: buying before the 3-second proof exists

If the benefit cannot be shown instantly in a silent vertical clip, the product needs more explanation than TikTok usually rewards.

Mistake 2: ignoring landed cost until after the sample

Cheap unit cost can disappear after shipping, platform fees, returns, creator cost, packaging, and refunds.

Mistake 3: trusting vague supplier pages

If variants, materials, package dimensions, MOQ, and real photos are unclear, the first sample may only teach you that the listing was vague.

Mistake 4: picking an account-risk product first

Electronics, ingestibles, beauty claims, medical-adjacent claims, kids products, and safety claims can add approval and compliance friction.

Mistake 5: having only one video angle

A first test needs repeatable clips: problem/solution, before/after, comparison, setup, mistake, routine, gift, or niche-specific use case.

Mistake 6: treating comments as guaranteed demand

Curiosity comments are not the same as purchase intent. Check whether the product solves a specific buyer problem at a believable price.

Mistake 7: copying a saturated winner without an angle

If the same product is everywhere, the question is not whether it can sell. The question is whether your version has a different buyer, bundle, hook, or proof moment.

Fast rule

If the idea needs long explanation, has thin margin, vague suppliers, or only one demo angle, reject it before the sample.
