Do not skip the pre-video gate

  • The buyer problem is visible without narration.
  • The product can be shown working in one clear shot.
  • The main objection can be answered visually.
  • Compliance and claim limits are written before scripting.
  • The test has a pass signal other than “we liked the video.”
Angle 1: Problem proof Show the annoying before-state so the right buyer self-identifies in the first second.
Angle 2: Demo proof Show the product solving one narrow problem with hands, scale, motion, or before/after evidence.
Angle 3: Objection proof Answer size, fit, durability, setup, cleaning, compatibility, or packaging doubts before comments do.

Worked example

Travel pill organizer UGC test map

TestFirst frameScript directionPass signal
Problem proof Loose supplement bottles filling a small travel bag. "If your weekend bag turns into a bottle drawer, this is the travel setup I would test first." Comments mention travel, gym bag, or daily meds without being prompted.
Demo proof One hand opens the organizer and shows labeled compartments. "Seven compartments, one snap, and the labels are visible before you leave." Viewers understand size and function with sound off.
Objection proof Organizer next to a palm, bottle, and bag pocket for scale. "Here is the actual size, what fits, and what I would not put inside it." Fewer questions about dimensions, fit, and expectations.
