Prelaunch checklist

Pass every row before you treat the product as launch-ready.

GatePass signalIf it fails
Buyer promiseA stranger can explain what the product does from the first image or first 3 seconds.Rewrite title, hook, and hero photo before launch.
Sample QCThe sample matches supplier claims, listing photos, variant details, and packaging expectations.Ask supplier for replacement proof or reject the product.
Photo proofMain image, scale shot, use-case shot, and detail shot all reduce buyer uncertainty.Reshoot before listing or route to the photo checklist.
Competitor price gapMargin survives the lowest believable competitor price plus returns and content-test budget.Pause supplier outreach or change bundle/angle.
First-order capThe first order is a proof batch, not a bulk inventory bet.Use the first-order calculator before paying MOQ.
Creator hookYou have problem, proof, and comparison hooks that do not need risky claims.Use the video hooks template before briefing anyone.
Return riskSize, fit, material, fragile packaging, and expectation gaps are handled in copy/photos.Fix the listing or reject until the sample answers it.

Simple rule: do not let one strong signal hide a weak gate. A good sample with bad margin, unclear photos, or no content hook is still not launch-ready.

Copyable prelaunch note

Paste this into your product folder before you publish.

Product: Buyer promise in one sentence: First 3-second proof shot: Sample QC result: Competitor floor price: Landed cost: Return or defect risk: First-order cap: Three creator hooks: Launch decision: launch / fix / reject

Reject faster: if the launch decision needs a long explanation, the listing is probably not ready. Fix proof, margin, or content before inventory.
