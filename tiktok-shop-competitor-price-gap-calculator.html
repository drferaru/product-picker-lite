Use this before paying for a sample

  • Find the lowest serious competitor, not the prettiest listing.
  • Subtract landed cost, selling fees, shipping support, and return reserve.
  • Keep budget for one small creator or AI video test before inventory.
  • Reject products that need fake scarcity, fake claims, or a mystery premium.
  • Only sample products where the gap survives a realistic first discount.

The price gap pass

Calculate the gap from market price backward.

Line itemWhat to enterDecision signal
Lowest believable competitor price Use a real listing with similar product, bundle, shipping promise, and review quality. If only weak listings are cheap, note why your product can justify a premium.
First discount price Subtract the launch coupon or discount you may need to get early traction. If the product only works at full price, pause before sampling.
Landed cost Unit cost plus domestic freight, international freight, packaging, tariffs or agent fees, and damaged-unit buffer. If landed cost is more than one third to one half of the realistic selling price, inspect hard.
Platform and payment friction Estimate selling fee, payment processing, shipping support, coupon cost, and any affiliate or creator commission. Do not assume fees disappear because the product is cheap.
Return and replacement reserve Add expected refunds, damaged items, size/fit mistakes, missing parts, and support burden. Products with fit, fragility, odor, safety, or expectation gaps need a bigger reserve.
Content-test budget Reserve cash for creator seeding, a small AI Product Video Test Pack, or first proof clips. If no money remains to test content, the product is not ready for inventory.

Decision rule: sample only if the remaining gap is still acceptable after a launch discount, realistic returns, and one content test. Otherwise find a cheaper source, stronger bundle, clearer angle, or different product.

Copyable worksheet

Paste this into notes before supplier outreach.

Product idea: Lowest serious competitor price: Realistic launch/discount price: Supplier unit cost: Estimated landed cost per unit: Platform/payment/shipping support estimate: Return/replacement reserve: Creator or AI video test reserve: Remaining price gap: Pass if: - The product still has margin after the first discount. - The supplier can explain cost, packaging, MOQ, and replacement policy. - The listing/video can prove why this version deserves the price. Reject or pause if: - Competitors already sell near landed cost. - The margin only works by ignoring returns or creator/content costs. - The product needs risky claims or fake urgency to justify price.

Do not win the supplier negotiation and lose the customer math. Cheap unit cost is not enough if buyers compare you against a low-price TikTok Shop listing with better proof, reviews, or shipping.
