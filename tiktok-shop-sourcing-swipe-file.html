Swipe 1

Supplier clarity message

SupplierSampleMOQ

Send this when the supplier's reply is friendly but still too vague to justify payment.

Hi [Supplier], before we pay for the sample, please confirm these six points in writing: 1. Sample unit price and shipping cost to [country]. 2. Exact SKU, size, color, material, and included parts. 3. Current packaging photos and label photos. 4. Lowest first-order quantity after the sample passes QC. 5. Replacement policy for wrong variant, missing parts, damage, or product not matching the listing. 6. Sample lead time and first small reorder lead time. If any point is not available yet, please mark it clearly. We cannot approve bulk quantity before the sample and content test are complete.

Pass signal: numbers, photos, terms, and timelines. Reject signal: “quality guaranteed,” “best price,” or MOQ pressure without details.

Swipe 2

Refund and defect-risk check

ReturnsDefectsCustomer complaints

Use this before a product with fragile parts, sizing uncertainty, electronics, leakage risk, or unclear included accessories.

Before we test this product, we need to understand likely customer complaints. Please answer: - What are the three most common defects or complaints for this SKU? - Which parts are most likely to arrive damaged, missing, or different from photos? - What proof do you need from us for a replacement or refund claim? - Can you send close-up photos of the packaging protection and included accessories? - If the first sample fails, do you replace it, refund it, or credit the next order? If replacement terms are not clear before payment, we will pause and compare other suppliers.

Do not ignore this: the most expensive weak products are not always the ones with bad margins. They are the ones that create refunds, bad reviews, and creator demos that hide the product's real failure mode.

Swipe 3

3-shot content proof before ordering

Creator hookDemo proofBefore/after

If a product cannot be explained in three shots, it may need a stronger angle or a different SKU before sample spend.

3-shot product proof: Shot 1 - Problem visible in one second: [show the annoying moment] Shot 2 - Product action: [show the product solving it without overexplaining] Shot 3 - Result check: [show the difference, cleanup, fit, storage, or convenience] Caption test: "If [specific buyer] keeps dealing with [specific problem], test this before buying the expensive version." If the product needs a long explanation, score it lower before ordering.
